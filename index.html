my mother's kimchi recipe

Ingredients Quantity
Napa cabbage 3 pounds (1.35 kg)
Kosher salt 1/4 c (36 g)
Water 1 c
Sweet rice flour (Mochiko) 1 T
Sugar 1 T
Korean radish or daikon,
cut into matchsticks		 1 c
Carrot, cut into matchsticks 1/2 c
scallions, chopped on bias 3
Asian chives (or 2 additional
scallions)		 1/2 c
garlic, minced 1/4 c
Ginger, minced 1 t
white onion, minced 1/2
Asian pear or apple, chopped 1/2
Korean hot pepper flakes
(Gochugaru)		 1 c
Fish sauce* 1/4 c
Saeujeot, fermented salty
shrimp with brine, chopped*		 3 T

* for a vegetarian version, you can omit the shrimp and substitute soy sauce or Braggs Amino Acid for the fish sauce.

Prepare the cabbage:

  • Remove outer leaves and trim cores from bottom if needed, then split in half lengthwise.
  • Wash thoroughly by rinsing in cold water, making sure to rinse the inner leaves. Repeat a few times if necessary.
  • Sprinkle the salt between the leaves by lifting up every leaf. Use more salt closer to the stems, where the leaves are thicker.
  • Let the cabbage rest for 2 hours, turning every 30 minutes or so, until the leaves have wilted.
  • Wash the cabbage again to remove excess salt. Cut the halves in two and cut the core out completely. Place in strainer to drain while you prepare the seasoning paste.

Prepare seasoning paste:

  • Combine the water and rice flour in a small pot over medium heat, mixing well to ensure smooth consistency. When the mixture starts to thicken and bubble, add sugar and cook for 1 more minute before removing from heat. Allow to cool while you prepare the other ingredients.
  • In a large bowl, add minced garlic, ginger, onion, apple, hot pepper flakes, fish sauce, and shrimp if using. Mix well. (alternately, you could put the onion, garlic, ginger, and apple into a food processor and create a paste)
  • Add rice flour mixture and carrot, radish, scallions, and chives. Mix well and taste for seasoning. It may need more salt, sugar, or fish sauce (if using).

Make the kimchi:

  • Spread some paste on each cabbage leaf until each quarter is coated. Fold each quarter in half and place in a container. Alternately, you can cut the cabbage into bite sized pieces and mix everything together before placing in container.

You can enjoy your kimchi right away. The kimchi will start fermenting a day or two at room temperature. You can also allow it to ferment at a slower rate in the refrigerator. Be sure to leave some space at the top of each container so the kimchi can release bubbles and juices during the fermentation process.

NOTES: Over time, the fermentation process will make the kimchi more sour tasting. As the flavor changes, your kimchi can also be used as an ingredient in a variety of traditional Korean dishes. Most of the Korean ingredients listed such as the sweet rice flour and salted shrimp can be found at Korean groceries, some Chinatown groceries, and online.
